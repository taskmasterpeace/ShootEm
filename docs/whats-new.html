v0.9.02026-07-21"The Air War → The Outbreak"

Everything between the afternoon of July 20 and the small hours of July 21: the sky got floors, the dead got a system, close combat became a contest — and the whole thing runs faster and downloads smaller than before.

New

  • The sky has floors. Aircraft fly in three altitude bands — Q climbs, E dives, and on the deck E is the door. Jets earn the sky: a real afterburner, and crossing the threshold visibly breaks the sound barrier — a vapor ring snaps off the fuselage and distant listeners hear the double-crack late.
  • Every gun is real. All weapon families have 3D models — six manufacturers change the silhouette, higher marks dress the barrel, and soldiers carry the exact gun they fire.
  • THE OUTBREAK — the dead are a system, not a spawn point:
    • Infection is separate from damage; untreated soldiers TURN — your own side, rising against you. Medics are the cure.
    • Hot bodies rise on a clock, by name, with a final-seconds warning. Fire, blasts, and bio-neutralizer deny them.
    • A sector Outbreak Level 0–4 climbs from CLEAR to SECTOR LOST from real conditions — bodies, bites, nests.
    • The body decides the form: scouts rise as sprinters, heavies as brutes. Sprinters lie dormant until light or noise wakes them — once.
    • Piled corpses curdle into contamination nests that mutate what rises there.
  • Seven ammunition types (tap B): Ball, Armor-Piercing, Incendiary, Tracer, Subsonic, Expanding, Bio-Neutralizer — each solves a different problem.
  • Close combat is a contest. STRIKE ▸ GRAPPLE ▸ GUARD ▸ STRIKE — knife (F), parrying guard (V), pinning grab (Z), and a held Impact Charge. A zombie's grab is a Bite Struggle: break free before the jaws close.
  • The downed experience speaks — a bleedout clock, a pulsing ring under fallen friendlies, a revive arc that closes as the medic works.
  • The HUD tells the truth — grenade pips, a visible spawn shield, MISSILE INBOUND + flares, the enemy god's drop countdown with a tightening landing ring.
  • The war remembers — clone print-counts, kill-streak notices, a flesh-vs-chrome ledger, a deadpan weather desk. And the game has its name: WAR WORLD: EARTH.

Improved

  • Dramatically better under load, proven on a public benchmark graph: the horde wall moved to ~790 zombies on screen, bot thinking got 26–37% cheaper, and multiplayer inputs queue per-client (no eaten presses).
  • The download shrank from 173MB to 47.6MB — audio re-encoded, nothing cut.

Fixed

  • Hover tags and enemy health rings now appear in offline matches too.
  • A live-playtest pass: vehicle hulls, flame, god balance, and meters that weren't drawing.
