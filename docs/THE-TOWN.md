# THE TOWN — what a soldier does when nobody is shooting

> Robert, 2026-07-23: *"I want you to audit the game and come up with some stuff
> we can have in town, outside of combat. What could they be doing? I already
> think I want them to be able to go to a paintball place… weapons testing and
> training certs. I really need you to be a creative partner and come up with
> some more stuff."*

---

## 1. THE AUDIT — what already exists

The striking finding: **all three things you named are already built.** They are
just menu entries instead of places.

| You said | It exists as | State |
|---|---|---|
| a paintball place | `paintball` mode — hunters vs hunted, the Gauntlet, **Vanessa's pro shop** (`/vanessas.html`, `shop` mode) | shipped |
| weapons testing | `range` mode + the **Threat Room** (20 preset experiments, the summon shelf) | shipped |
| training certs | **The Schools** — 12 certifications, 12 driving courses, a real licence register that now gates the wheel | shipped |

And more that nobody can walk to:

| Surface | What it is | Reached by |
|---|---|---|
| The Garage | tyres/engines/chassis/cargo, real fits that change how a hull drives | a menu |
| The Circuit | 9 track pieces, a track builder, lap records | a menu |
| The Threat Room | the combat laboratory | a menu |
| The Codex | every weapon, hull, licence and now cargo | a tab |
| The GONET | desk, briefings, mail, broadcast, music, your file | the front door |
| The Board | lap times per track and class | inside a file |

**So the gap is not content. It is CONNECTIVE TISSUE.** Six real places exist and
the player reaches every one of them through a list. There is no *town* — no
ground you stand on, no door you walk through, nobody to see.

That is the thing to build, and it reframes everything below: the answer to
"what could they be doing" is **a place with doors**, and most of the doors are
already made.

---

## 2. THE PROPOSAL — the town as a hub you walk around

One small map. No enemies, no timer, no score. You spawn on a street. Buildings
have doors, and a door is how you enter something that already exists.

This is worth doing for three reasons beyond the walking:

1. **It makes the procedural city generator load-bearing.** You asked for
   buildings that fit the generated town — a hub is the one map where the
   player is *inside* the architecture at walking pace, looking at it. It is
   the honest test of whether the buildings are good.
2. **It gives the account somewhere to be spent.** Rank, licences, the war
   chest, the garage and the record board are all real now and all live in
   menus. A town gives each of them a counter and a person behind it.
3. **It is where other people are.** When accounts land (#83), a town is the
   only place in this game where two players could stand next to each other
   without one of them being a target.

---

## 3. THE SLATE — doors, ranked

### Tier 1 — the doors that already have a room behind them

These are cheap: the room is built, the door is new.

1. **THE YARD** — paintball. Walk in, Vanessa's pro shop is the counter, the
   Gauntlet is out back. *Exists.*
2. **THE RANGE** — weapons testing. Every gun on the wall, a lane, and paper.
   *Exists* (`range` mode). Add: **zeroing** — sight in an optic and it stays
   sighted; a **qualification badge** per weapon family, which is a real thing
   to hang on a service file.
3. **THE SCHOOLS** — the motor pool, armour school, naval yard, flight school,
   signals school. Five real schools, twelve certifications. *Exists.* A door
   each, and the buildings should look like what they teach.
4. **THE GARAGE** — your machines, your fits. *Exists.* Give it a lift, a
   parts wall, and the noise of a shop.
5. **THE CIRCUIT** — the track builder and the record board. *Exists.*
6. **THE THREAT ROOM** — the lab, in a bunker on the edge of town.

### Tier 2 — new rooms that make existing systems visible

7. **THE MESS / THE BAR.** Where the roster becomes people. The canon's open
   item is *"what friendships/rivalries DO mechanically"* — this is where you
   find out who you keep ending up next to. It also has the food-truck effect
   with a roof on it: men who eat hold their nerve (morale is a real stat now).
8. **THE MEMORIAL.** The names of prints that died. The newspaper already files
   obituaries and MIA notices; this is where they land. In a game about
   *prints* — where death is not permanent for the account but is permanent for
   the body — a wall of names is the strongest thing in the whole town.
9. **THE CLINIC / THE PRINT SHOP.** Where a new print is authorised and walks
   out. Intake already says *"watch it being manufactured."* Put the machine in
   a building the player can visit again.
10. **THE PROMOTION BOARD.** A room with a desk and an officer. The board is
    real now (rank read off service, authorities granted). Being *told* you
    made Lieutenant by somebody, in a room, is worth ten times a number in a
    file.
11. **THE POST OFFICE.** GONET mail has a physical counter. Letters home — the
    hometown notice already writes to you; this is where you write back.
12. **THE MARKETPLACE.** Canon GONET item, unbuilt. The war chest currently
    only *sets* your manifest; a market is where money is spent on things you
    keep.

### Tier 3 — the creative swings

13. **THE HOVERBOARD PARK.** You have a full trick economy — grinds, wall
    rides, boost jumps, bail-or-bank landings — and nowhere built for it. A
    park with authored kickers, rails and a bowl turns a movement system into a
    *place*. Best score of the day on a board by the gate.
14. **THE CINEMA.** GONET BROADCAST already cuts your battles into reels with a
    real transport. Put them on a screen, in a dark room, with other people's
    reels playing between yours. The war watching itself.
15. **THE RADIO STATION.** The music library exists and plays through your
    headphones on the field. A station is where the tracks come from — request
    one, hear it in tomorrow's deployment, and a DJ who reads the news off the
    press issues you generated.
16. **THE IMPOUND / THE BREAKER'S YARD.** Wrecks go somewhere. Salvage a wreck
    for garage parts. The war ledger already counts every hull your side lost —
    this is that number as a field of dead machines you can walk through.
17. **THE JOB BOARD (THE LOCKER).** Canon: *the locker is bounty life, not
    loss-punishment.* A board of one-off contracts — recover a hull, escort a
    scientist, win a race — posted by the world rather than the war.
18. **THE ARMOURY COUNTER.** Not a menu: a person behind a cage who hands you
    what your papers and rank entitle you to, and refuses what they do not.
    The refusal is the game (the licence gate is real now).
19. **THE GYM / OBSTACLE COURSE.** The 8 master stats are canon. A course that
    trains one is honest progression that is *not* a number going up in a menu.
20. **THE BARBER / THE INK.** Appearance is an intake phase already. Unit
    insignia, a scar you keep, a print that looks like the last one on purpose.

### The one I would build first

**THE MEMORIAL**, and it is not close. Every other door leads to an activity;
that one leads to a *feeling*, and it costs almost nothing — the obituary data
already exists. A town where your dead prints have names is a town the player
will come back to for reasons the design did not have to manufacture.

## 4. ONBOARDING WITH WEIGHT

> *"Right now you just enter your name and you just choose a bunch of flags.
> It's hard to really tell what you're selecting, and I don't know what stats
> are important to your home city."*

### What was wrong

169 nations rendered as a flat grid of flags with a faction dot. The data behind
each one is genuinely rich — population, government, perception, motto, leader,
cloning law, LSW regulation, and four doctrine stats (military · intel ·
science · lswActivity) — **and not one number of it was on screen.** The city
list has existed the whole time, with a comment saying the enlistee picks a
hometown from it, and nothing ever asked.

### The answer to "what stats matter"

Two different questions, and they deserve two different answers:

> **THE COUNTRY DECIDES WHAT YOUR ARMY IS.**
> **THE CITY DECIDES WHAT YOU ARE.**

**The country** sets your faction (already derived from its doctrine) and the
character of the war you are joining. A nation heavy in *military* fields more
armour; heavy in *science* fields better kit; heavy in *lswActivity* means gods
walk more often where you are from. Its *perception* (Full Democracy →
Authoritarian Regime) and its *cloning* and *LSW* laws say what kind of place
made you — and in a game about prints, a country that has **banned cloning** is
a genuinely different origin than one where it is legal.

**The city** gives you a **HOMETOWN**, and a hometown is a head start. Every
city resolves to an archetype, and the archetype hands you two of the
twenty-two secondary skills already in the game:

| Archetype | You grew up around | Head start |
|---|---|---|
| PORT | water, cranes, salt | Boat · Navigator |
| INDUSTRIAL | shift horns and machine shops | Mechanic · Engineer |
| CAPITAL | offices, parades, paperwork | Commander · Radio Operator |
| GARRISON | a base at the edge of town | Rifle · Discipline |
| FRONTIER | distance, weather, self-reliance | Scout · Navigator |
| UNIVERSITY | labs and lecture halls | Engineer · Medic |
| MINING | charges, dust, dark | Explosives · Mechanic |
| FARM | rifles, engines, patience | Rifle · Scout |
| TRANSPORT | depots, yards, long hauls | Tank Driver · Mechanic |

The archetype is **derived**, not hand-authored for 800 cities: the country's
own doctrine stats, the city's position in its nation's list (the first-listed
city leans capital), and a stable hash of the name. Same city, same origin,
every time — and it means the choice on the intake screen is a real choice with
a mechanical consequence you can read before you make it.

This also answers the deeper thing you were circling: the reason picking a flag
felt weightless is that **nothing downstream ever read it**. Now the country
picks your side and colours your war, and the city puts two skills in your
hands on day one.

## 5. What this unlocks next

- The town hub map is the natural home for the **procedural city generator** —
  and the honest test of whether its buildings are good, because the player is
  walking through them at eye level instead of driving past at 40.
- Hometown archetypes give the **newspaper** and **mail** something local to
  say ("the yards at home are on strike").
- When accounts arrive (#83), the town is where two players can be in the same
  place without one of them being a target.
